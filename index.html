Dear Papa,

Happy 70th birthday. 🎂🎁❤️

It actually feels strange writing that number — 70. In my mind you're still the same Papa I grew up with… just with your super vision that you have now after the cataract surgery, which you are extremely proud of. 😆

When I think about growing up, one thing that always stands out about you is how quietly you did everything for the family. You were never the loud type. If anything, most of the time the complaints probably went to Mama first, and then Mama would deliver the message to us. 😏

But somehow, things were always taken care of. We always had what we needed. When we were younger we didn’t realise how much work and responsibility was behind that. Now that I’m older, I understand it more.

One thing I've always loved about you is your sense of humour. Even in serious conversations you somehow manage to throw in a comment that makes everyone laugh. It's one of those things that made home feel lighter.

And then of course… the onions! 🧅🧅😱 Every time I pick onions out from my food, you give me that look. The same look and same nagging, every time. I totally get it! Because to you, onions are basically one of the greatest foods ever created — antioxidants, nutrients, health benefits, tasty, miracles… I don’t think I’ve met anyone else defended onions as passionately as you. But yea...still not into it. 🤪

Some of my favourite memories with you are the really random ones.

Like your famous tug-of war with Lubby when he tried to steal the durian. Though you don’t like durian, but when Lubby grabbed the durian you immediately went over and went into a ended up in a championship match with him. I still remember watching the two of you pulling on opposite sides of the durian. It was chaotic, ridiculous, and absolutely unforgettable. 🤭

Also, the swings you built for us at the 245 house. That’s one of those small things that probably seemed simple to you, but to us it meant a lot. We spent lot of happy hours on those swings.

Then there’s your “signature dish” — Spam with onions and dark sauce. Even though I’ve never tried that recipe from your cooking, somehow this combination has become a family classic. 😋

Another thing I admire about you is your passion for music 🎼🎹🎵🎶 🎚️ When you were serving as a keyboardist in church every weekend, you were super committed to it. You practised seriously and showed up faithfully. I could always see how much joy it brought you.

At the same time, you are also someone who knows exactly what you like. Your favourite restaurants, your favourite dishes — once you decide they are the best in town, that’s it. And every time you bring us there, you introduce it with so much confidence and passion that it always makes us laugh.

And when it comes to certain things, your focus can be… quite legendary. Like chasing a cat in the warehouse and ending up breaking your finger. Or the period of time when you were researching all those fake dead bird props to scare birds away from the warehouse. When you decide something needs to be done, you really go all in.

When I think about all these little memories, they may seem small on their own. But together they form the picture of the Papa we grew up with. ❤️

Someone steady. Someone dependable. Someone who quietly worked hard so the family could move forward.

When we were young, we probably didn’t think about these things much. Life just felt normal. But now that I’m older, I realise how much of that “normal” was actually quietly shaped by you.

So today, on your 70th birthday, I just want to say something simple:

Thank you.

Thank you for the life you built for our family. Thank you for all the effort that we didn’t see when we were younger. Thank you for all the little moments that became some of our happiest memories.

Sometimes fathers don't say a lot. But their love shows up in the things they do.

Looking back now, I realise that your love has been there all along — in all the quiet ways you took care of us.

And Papa, I hope you know this: The things you did mattered. They shaped our lives more than you probably realise.

Happy 70th birthday, Papa. ❤️

I'm really grateful that you're my father.

Love,
Xiao Yen

Happy 70th Birthday, Papa! 🎂

Seventy years. That's a number worth celebrating — and I, your son of 38 years, feel honoured to have experienced life with you for most of it.

If I had to describe you in one line, it would be this: a man who never worried a day in his life, yet somehow dependable and responsible. Carefree on the outside, quietly dependable on the inside. That, Papa, is a superpower.

🏠 The Ceiling Incident (A Trauma Report)
It all started when I was four years old. You, in your infinite wisdom, decided that throwing your children toward the ceiling was a perfectly reasonable form of entertainment. First up: Xiao Ting — who, to her credit, laughed and asked for more. Encouraged by this response, you then launched me skyward.
I screamed. I cried. You laughed.
Papa, I want you to know that I have recovered. Mostly. But it does explain why I am the Kiasi boy from young. You made me who I am.

🚗 The World's Most Dedicated Driver
For years, you were our personal driver — ferrying us to tuition, to the swimming pool on weekends (which I genuinely loved, by the way), and to Johor Bahru for shopping. You never complained about a single kilometre.

But my absolute favourite driving memory? The day you came to pick me up from my old primary school… when I was already in secondary school. Mum and I laughed until our sides hurt. You, of course, remained calm and laughed as well— which somehow made it even funnier. Classic Papa.

🥦 The Vegetable Negotiations
You made us eat our vegetables, even when you clearly had to force yourself to eat them too — just to lead by example. The look on your face said "I do not want this" but your actions said "I am your father and we are doing this together." That, Papa, is love.

🎸 The Things Only You Can Do
Mum is, without question, a supermom. But there are things that belong only to you. The way you picked up the keyboard in your 40s, just because you wanted to. The guitar. The harmonica. I am secretly proud being your son on those days that you perform keyboard in front of whole the church.

🧘 The Art of Surviving Mum
I have never once seen you lose your cool under fire. When Mum is in full force, you have a gift — a well-timed compliment, a little distraction, maybe something happens in front — and just like that, the storm passes and you're somehow smiling again. Papa, that is not just patience. That is a mastery that I am still studying.

👨‍👧‍👧 Now That I'm a Father Too
Since becoming a father of two daughters, I have begun to truly understand the weight of what you carried all those years. The exhaustion of keeping kids occupied on weekends. The loss of any personal space or quiet. The midnight disruptions, the doctor visits, the relentless giving of yourself — day after day, year after year, for twenty years.

You never complained. Not once.

You were not the kind of dad who measured love in achievements or report cards. You were the kind who simply showed up, again and again, loving us for exactly who we are. That kind of love is rare, Papa. And I do not take it for granted.

For all of it — the ceiling throws, the vegetable standoffs, the years behind the wheel, the music, the laughter, the steadiness — thank you. I give thanks to the Lord for the specific gift of having you as my Papa.

We may be separated by the Causeway, and yes, crossing with two young kids is basically a challenge. But I promise to keep making the trip and you and Mum are always welcome to visit us in Singapore anytime — because your granddaughters deserve to grow up knowing you, and because there are more memories still to be made. More weekend trips. More laughter. More of your cooking critiques on my dishes. More life, beyond the YouTube doomscrolling.

Here's to 70 years of you, Papa. And many, many more. 🥂

With all my love,
Eng Yao
